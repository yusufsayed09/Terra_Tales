The mission confirmed the significant, long-term global decline in CO concentrations, particularly in the industrialized Northern Hemisphere, proving that strict environmental policies and technological improvements were highly effective in cleaning up traditional man-made pollution. This progress has been increasingly challenged by severe, acute pollution spikes from immense wildfires (e.g., in Russia, Australia, and Canada). These events demonstrate that climate-driven combustion is now the dominant air quality threat. Terra's "eyes" ultimately documented a fundamental shift in risk: from chronic, predictable industrial smog to severe, episodic, and globally transported smoke events that require unified international policy to address.
